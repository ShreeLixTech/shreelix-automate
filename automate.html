Help & FAQ

What's the difference between "Gmail" and "Bulk Merge" connections?
Gmail (in the Connections tab) lets you send single messages to one person at a time in Compose & Send. Bulk Merge is a separate, broader connection needed only if you want to email everyone in a Google Sheet at once, optionally attaching a personalized document from Google Slides.
What are the {{curly braces}} for?
They're placeholders. If your Sheet has a column called "name", writing {{name}} anywhere in your subject or message gets automatically replaced with that person's actual name when it sends — a different value for every row.
Why do I need a Slides template with placeholders?
For "Bulk + document" sends, you design one document once (e.g. a certificate) with {{placeholders}} matching your Sheet's columns. We make a personalized copy for every row automatically, so you never design it more than once.
My scheduled message didn't send. What do I check?
First, check the Schedules tab — does "Last run" show a real date, or still "Never run yet"? Then check the History tab for any failed entries. If both are empty, wait a little longer — schedules are checked every few minutes, not instantly at the exact second.
Why did my email land in spam?
This depends on the recipient's email provider, not something we control directly. Using your real Gmail account (not a brand-new one) and avoiding spammy language in your subject line both help.
What happens when my 100 free credits run out?
You'll see an upgrade screen where you can pay ₹299 instantly by card, UPI, or netbanking through Razorpay — your account upgrades automatically the moment payment completes, no waiting. Manual call/WhatsApp options are also available if you'd rather pay that way.
Is there a limit to how many people I can bulk-send to at once?
Gmail itself limits regular accounts to roughly 500 sends per day. For very large lists, consider splitting sends across a few days.
